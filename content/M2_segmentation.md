## 2. Core argument (draft)

**One mode does not win every grocery mission.** Use **distance × basket × urgency** to route: **sidewalk robots** own **short, hub-and-spoke, small-to-medium baskets** where operator envelopes already prove fit (**~≤2 mi** Starship framing; **~half of US delivery distances &lt;2.5 mi** Serve 10-K narrative). **Drones** own **light, time-sensitive** missions inside **~2.5 lb / 12 mi RT** Wing-class envelopes **where airspace and landing work**—**phase-gated** after ground baseline. **Humans** retain **heavy weekly shops, long legs, fragile handoffs, bad weather, and restricted SKUs**. **Rollout ODD** matches **campus / micro-geofence first** (Bird/Lime density playbook → **GMU**), then expand on gates—not open residential sprawl on day one.

## 3. Opinionated stance (S2-2) — **Robots first; aerial in-pilot but gated**

| Principle | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Primary volume = sidewalk** | Lower regulatory surface than ubiquitous BVLOS; Starship/Serve/Uber path is **repeatable**; aligns **named campus** pilot. |
| **Aerial = included but gated** | Satisfies case “aerial + sidewalk” without making **go/no-go** depend on drone SLAs on **Day 0**. Open **Lane B** after **0–30** readiness (see **M7/M8**). |
| **Human = retain zone** | Default for **&gt;2–2.5 mi** heavy baskets, **alcohol/ID**, **extreme weather**, **complex access** (S2-8, S2-7). |

## 5. Operator envelopes (evidence layer for matrix)

### Sidewalk — Starship (FAQ + ordering)

- **Distance:** “Generally work within a **2 mile** distance from stores.”
- **Time:** **~22 min** avg store-to-door (cities) — v3 strategy; earlier extract also cited **~4 mph** / **~20 min** — **VERIFY** single line for deck.
- **Basket:** Up to **three grocery bags** (not refrigerated/heated; insulated lining).
- **Go / no-go:** Outside **~2 mi** or **&gt; three-bag** framing → **no-go** for robot-primary.

### Sidewalk — Serve (2023 Form 10-K)

- **Market shape:** **~half** of US delivery distances **&lt;2.5 mi**; suited to sidewalk robots (p.1–2).
- **Payload:** Up to **50 lbs**; max speed **7 mph** (p.4).
- **Throughput anchor:** Robots **12 hr/day** → **max 20–30 deliveries/day** (theoretical batched) (p.4).
- **QA:** Quote **exact** 10-K language in final PDF (strategy v3 flagged spot-check).

### Aerial — Wing

- **Payload ~2.5 lb**; **12 mi round trip**; **65 mph** cruise; consumer-facing **as little as ~30 min**.
- **Go:** Small, time-bound SKUs where **landing / customer handoff** works.
- **No-go:** **Full weekly shop**, many **dense urban** handoffs **until** ops design + airspace gates proven.

### Airspace gating

- **FAA:** Multiple operators **BVLOS** in **Dallas** area with UTM, below 400 ft (**Jul 30, 2024** newsroom) — **progress**, not universal permission.

## 8. Real sidewalk & geography constraints (S2-4, S2-7)

**Honest friction (PM maturity):**

- **Road crossings, curb cuts, construction, crowding** → stuck-bot taxonomy; **human tele-ops / fallback** (→ **M6/M7**).
- **Weather:** Snow, ice, flooding — **degrade robot SLA**; **human retain** or **delay**.
- **Terrain:** Gravel, steep hills, poor mapping — **no-go** until mapped.
- **Airspace:** Airports, TFRs, dense urban canyons — **drone no-go** without specific approvals.
- **Zoning / campus rules:** **Loading zones**, **robot lanes**, **time-of-day** caps — **part of pilot permits**.

## 9. Order-profile & compliance filters (S2-8)

| Filter | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| **Perishables / temp** | Robot **insulated, not refrigerated** (Starship FAQ) — **limit** ultra-temp-sensitive SKUs in pilot menu or **human** lane. |
| **Fragile / eggs** | **Packaging SLOs**; may **exclude** from autonomous in v1. |
| **Alcohol** | **ID verification** at handoff — often **human retain** or **locker** with **app check**; **state-dependent** — **pilot: exclude or restricted hours**. |
| **High-value / fraud** | **PIN / unlock** flow (→ **M4** mocks). |
