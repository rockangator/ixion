## 2. Core argument (draft)

Instacart’s long-term margin and reliability are **last-mile constrained**: human fulfillment stays **expensive** (~**$8–12/order** modeled baseline aligned with finance workstream—not a single GAAP “last-mile only” filing line), while **gig classification and local minimum-pay rules** keep repricing risk live. At the same time, **sidewalk and aerial autonomy are scaling through partners** (Serve/Uber, Starship, Wing), and **Amazon Scout retrenched**—so “wait and see” cedes learning and partnerships to competitors. **Invest now** in a **partner-first, geofenced, economics-gated** program; **prove** trust and unit cost in a **named campus pilot** (GMU / Fairfax — see `00_checklist_map.md`) before open-city scale.

## 5. On-slide bullets — business column (dense)

Use **3–5** on the slide; rest → appendix.

- **Cost anchor (explicit assumption):** Model **~$8–12 fully loaded human last-mile / order** as shared baseline across deck; **Instacart does not publish one clean GAAP “fulfillment $/order” for last mile only**—finance (INS-6 / **M5**) owns reconciliation. *(v3 strategy + QA note.)*
- **Gig / classification pressure:** USDOL **FLSA employee vs. independent contractor** rule published **Jan 10, 2024**, effective **Mar 11, 2024** (Federal Register **2024-00067**).
- **Scaled autonomy — ground:** Serve **10-K**: Uber agreement **June 2022** to deploy up to **2,000** robots (goal **EOY 2025**). *(SEC filing.)*
- **Scaled autonomy — ops proof:** Starship **270+** locations, **9M+** deliveries, up to **three** grocery bags, **~22 min** avg store-to-door (cities). *(Starship FAQ / ordering materials.)*
- **Scaled autonomy — air:** Wing consumer envelope **~2.5 lb** payload, **12 mi** round trip, **65 mph** cruise; **as little as ~30 min** on consumer-facing materials. *(Wing learn / get-delivery.)*
- **Regulatory readiness (air):** FAA **BVLOS** milestone — multiple commercial operators in **Dallas** area with UTM, below 400 ft (**Jul 30, 2024** FAA newsroom).
- **Cautionary precedent:** **Amazon Scout** live tests ended **Oct 2022** — hardware programs without durable customer fit get cut. *(Reuters.)*
- **Operational proof $/order matters (optional fourth bullet):** Walmart reported **~40%** reduction in U.S. delivery cost per order from fulfillment efficiency work. *(Grocery Dive — from earlier extract; VERIFY link live.)*

## 6. North star — “eliminate dashers” (S1-4)

**On-slide (one line, careful):** Long-term fulfillment leverage lives in **removing the variable-cost human last-mile leg** in **fit zones**—after trust, regulation, and economics gates.

**Speaker / M6 bridge:** Not “fire shoppers.” **Reality:** picking + exception handling + fleet ops **remain human-heavy**; gig last-mile **contracts** in autonomous-eligible routes. **Workforce transition + community risk** = **M6** (S7-5).
